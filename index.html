What is the probability that I get in my dream graduate school given my status and my GPA?


In the picture above, the parameters of the gaussian are used to model how hard it is to get in the school (a high
GPA and a small deviance for example, indicates a hard college to get into). I use this gaussian model paired with
the probability of an international student getting in the particular school to design a model to predict the chance
of a random student to get in a top school just given the these two variables. The values of parameters mu and sigma are
fixed given the university considered by the student.

I used the data provided by gradcafe to make the model and the prediction.

Particle Swarm optimization to learn best parameters for 3D environment sniper
I did research during the Fall semester with Dr.Dellinger about ways to lead a 3D sniper agent to best position himself in order
to make the most damages. We use a stochastic gradientless method called Particle Swarm Optimization to search the space of parameters
by making the agents fight a set of static agents (static in order to keep track of the improvement of the swarm).
Results were pretty impressive. We were able to find not only the weights of the best agents but also
the best spots in the map for a sniper to do very well. Below I included a picture of the heatmap of winning sniper.
Learn more about it on my github repo here.
